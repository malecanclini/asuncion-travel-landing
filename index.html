Why Asunción?

Asunción, the Mother of Cities

Founded in 1537, Asunción is one of the oldest cities in South America. It is known as the "Mother of Cities" because it was the starting point for colonial expeditions that founded more than 70 cities across the region.

Today, you can still see traces of history on every corner. Beyond the modern shopping malls and new skyscrapers, the historic center preserves its colorful facades, cobblestone streets, and the iconic "Lapacho" trees that paint the city in pink and yellow during winter. Walking along the Costanera at sunset, seeing the illuminated Lopez Palace, and feeling the warmth of its people is a truly unique experience.

My favorite spots in Asunción

Loma San Jerónimo

A picturesque neighborhood full of colors, narrow passages, and an incredible view of the port.

Address: San Jerónimo Neighborhood, Asunción.

What I like about it:

It's super photogenic! Getting lost in its alleys and having a drink on the terraces while watching the river is the best part.

El Café de Acá

A cozy place that feels like being in the backyard of a traditional Paraguayan home.

Address: Tte. Vera & Dr. Morra.

What I like about it:

They have the best "cocido quemado" and "mbeju" in town. The atmosphere is relaxed and the staff is very friendly.
